1938

Albert Hofmann was a Swiss chemist working at Sandoz Laboratories. While working at Sandoz, Hofmann studied the medical plant Drama maritime as well as ergot fungus, as part of a program to synthesize and purify the active constituents to be used as pharmaceuticals. When experimenting on stimulants, accidentally synthesized lysergic acid diethylamide (LSD).

Five years after Hofmann first synthesizes LSD, he accidentally absorbed a small amount of the drug through his finger tip. A few days later, Hofmann began self-experimenting with the drug. Assuming he was taking a threshold dose*, Hofmann experienced a sudden change in perception within an hour.

By 1949, Sandoz Laboratory started distributing LSD under the name of Delysid for psychiatric use and research.
